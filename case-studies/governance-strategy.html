Access Governance Strategy & Implementation

Strategic roadmap and implementation of comprehensive Identity Governance & Administration framework for a large government organization.

Industry: Government
Location: India
Duration: 12 Months
Team Size: 6 Consultants

Project Overview

A government organization managing critical national infrastructure needed to establish a robust Identity Governance & Administration (IGA) framework to ensure compliance, reduce security risks, and streamline access management across 200+ applications.

The engagement involved developing a multi-year IGA strategy, selecting appropriate technologies, and implementing the initial phase of access governance capabilities.

Key Highlights

3-year strategic roadmap developed
200+ applications assessed
Automated access certification
75% reduction in access review time

Challenges

Fragmented Access Landscape

No centralized view of user access across 200+ applications with different authentication mechanisms and access models.

Manual Compliance Processes

Time-consuming manual access reviews taking 6+ months to complete, with poor documentation and audit trails.

Regulatory Pressure

Increasing regulatory requirements for access governance and audit readiness with limited resources and expertise.

Our Approach

1

Current State Assessment

  • • Comprehensive inventory of all applications and access patterns
  • • Risk assessment of critical applications and data
  • • Analysis of existing access review processes and pain points
  • • Regulatory and compliance requirements mapping
2

Strategic Roadmap Development

  • • 3-year phased implementation roadmap
  • • Technology selection and architecture design (SailPoint IdentityIQ)
  • • Governance framework design with roles and responsibilities
  • • Business case and ROI analysis
3

Phase 1: Foundation Implementation

  • • SailPoint IdentityIQ deployment and configuration
  • • Integration with 50 priority applications
  • • Identity data aggregation and correlation
  • • Automated access certification workflows
4

Role-Based Access Control Design

  • • Business role mining and definition for critical applications
  • • Entitlement catalog creation and management
  • • Segregation of Duties (SoD) policy definition
  • • Access request and approval workflows
5

Compliance & Reporting

  • • Automated quarterly access certification campaigns
  • • Audit-ready compliance reports and dashboards
  • • SoD violation detection and remediation
  • • Policy enforcement and exception management
6

Change Management & Training

  • • Stakeholder engagement and communication plan
  • • Role-based training for administrators, reviewers, and end users
  • • Process documentation and standard operating procedures
  • • Center of Excellence (CoE) establishment

Business Benefits

  • Reduced access certification cycle from 6 months to 6 weeks
